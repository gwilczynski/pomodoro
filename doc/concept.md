# Time Timer

## What is a Time Timer?
A Time Timer is a specialized visual timer designed to make the passage of time intuitive and easy to understand. Unlike traditional digital or analog clocks that rely on numbers or the movement of small hands, a Time Timer uses a disappearing colored disk (typically red) to represent time remaining.

## How It Works
The Visual Disk: You turn a center dial or knob to set the desired amount of time (most classically, up to 60 minutes). This pulls a brightly colored disk across the face of the timer.

Time Elapsing: As time passes, the disk gradually disappears in a counter-clockwise direction.

The Concept: It physically shows how much time is left as a tangible, shrinking slice of a circle. When the color is gone, time is up.

## Primary Uses and Benefits
Because it translates the abstract concept of time into a concrete, visual representation, it is widely used across several domains:

Neurodiversity and Accessibility: It is highly recommended for individuals with ADHD, Autism, or executive dysfunction. It helps reduce the anxiety associated with "time blindness" and makes managing transitions between activities much easier.

Education and Parenting: Teachers and parents use it to establish routines, limit screen time, and show children exactly how long they have to complete a chore or wait for an event, even if the children cannot read a traditional clock yet.

Productivity: Professionals and students use it for time-blocking techniques (like the Pomodoro Technique). It facilitates deep work by providing a quick, non-distracting visual cue of time remaining without the need to read numbers or calculate minutes.
